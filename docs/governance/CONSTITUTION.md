# IdeaCapital Governance Constitution

> **Status:** Draft (v1.0)
> **Effective Date:** TBD

## Preamble

We, the community of IdeaCapital, establish this Constitution to govern the decentralized allocation of capital to scientific invention. Our mission is to democratize deep tech funding through transparent, liquid, and expert-curated decision making.

## Article I: The Reputation Token (REP)

### Section 1. Definition
The Reputation Token (REP) is a non-transferable (Soulbound) ERC-20 token that represents a member's expertise and contribution to the network.

### Section 2. Utility
REP grants the right to:
1.  Submit Governance Proposals.
2.  Vote on Governance Proposals.
3.  Curate inventions (signal novelty).

### Section 3. Acquisition
REP is earned by:
1.  **Inventors:** Minted upon successful funding of an IP-NFT (1 REP per $100 raised).
2.  **Backers:** Minted upon early backing of successful projects (1 REP per $500 invested).
3.  **Curators:** Minted for identifying Prior Art that invalidates a submission (Bounty program).

---

## Article II: Decision Making (Liquid Democracy)

### Section 1. Delegation
Any REP holder may delegate their voting power to another address. Delegation is revocable at any time. This allows passive members to empower domain experts.

### Section 2. Proposal Threshold
To submit a proposal to the DAO, a member must hold a minimum of **100 REP**.

### Section 3. Voting Parameters
*   **Voting Period:** 7 days.
*   **Quorum:** A proposal is valid only if **10%** of the total circulating REP supply participates.
*   **Majority:** Simple majority (>50%) of participating votes.

---

## Article III: Amendment Process

This Constitution may be amended by a Governance Proposal that achieves a **Supermajority (66%)** and a Quorum of **20%**.
